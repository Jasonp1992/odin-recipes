Shepard's Pie

  • Home

Description

White fluffy mashed potatoes topping a rich mix of gravy meat and vegetables. This is probable one of my easiest recipes. perfect for an easy week night supper. This is a great recipe for people new or learning how to cook.

Ingredients

  • 1 Pound Ground Beef*
  • 1 White Onion
  • 1 Cup Frozen Peas
  • 1 Cup Frozen Corn
  • 2 Cups Sliced Bella Mushrooms
  • 1 Can Cambals Cream of Mushroom Soup
  • 1 Packet Powdered Au Jus Sauce
  • 5 Large Russet Potatoes
  • 5 Tablespoons Salted Butter
  • 1/4 Cup milk (or milk substitute)
  • 1 Tablespoon Olive Oil
  • Shredded Cheddar Cheese (for topping if desired)

* I like to use a leaner beef but feel free to use any beef you like.

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil
  2. While you wait of the water to boil, cut potatoes into one inch cubes and wash till the wash water runs clear
  3. Once the water is boiling add the potatoes to the water and cook until a fork can easily peirce the potatoes
  4. Drain water form the potatoes and return them to the pot they were cooking in
  5. Add the butter and milk to the potatoes along with a pinch of salt and pepper and mash till the potatoes are smooth
  6. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed, Put the potatoes aside for now till we have the sauce finished
  7. In a 10 inch cast iron pan or other oven safe pan, heat one tablespoon olive oil
  8. Once pan is hot add the ground beef, once the beef starts to brown use a spatula to brake up the beef into small chunks
  9. Turn on you ovens broiler to medium and let it warm up
  10. Once the beef is just about done cooking drain off any liquid that might have accumulated in the pan
  11. return the pan to the heat and add the onions and mushrooms, saute until the onions are soft and translucent and the mushrooms have softened
  12. Add the can of cream of mushroom soup and half of the can of water, stir until the soup is smooth and mixed well with the meat and vegetables
  13. Add the packet of au jus sauce, frozen corn, and frozen peas to the pan, stir till well mixed
  14. Cook over medium heat till hot
  15. Removed the pot from the heat, smooth out the sauce so its evenly distributed in the pan
  16. Scoop the potatoes and spread them on top of the sauce, this is a little tricky a good idea is to take small scoops and dollop it on top of the sauce
  17. At this point if you would like to add cheese, sprinkle the cheese on top of the mashed potatoes
  18. Now take your pan with everything in it and put it under the broiler until the cheese is melted and the potatoes have started to brown. If you did not add the cheese I still put it under the broiler till the mash potatoes have started to brown. But if you don't want to do this step it can be skipped
  19. Scoop out into a bowl or a plate and its ready to serve optionally garnish with fresh chopped parsley
